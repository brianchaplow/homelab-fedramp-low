Three intentional state transitions were staged in DefectDojo before
running the May cycle, per ADR 0010 scope decision #1:

1. **Closed via remediation:** Finding id=5 `CVE-2026-32249 in Vim`
   was patched across all in-boundary Ubuntu 24.04 hosts via
   `apt upgrade`. Marked `is_mitigated=True, active=False` in
   DefectDojo. Shows as `poam-state: Completed` in the May OSCAL output.

2. **New finding introduced:** Finding id=16945 `CVE-2026-99999 in
   Libfoo on brisket (synthetic May cycle demo)` was created directly
   in DefectDojo with `date=2026-04-25`, representing a new
   vulnerability discovered between cycles. Severity: Medium. Shows
   as a new `Open` item in the May OSCAL output.

3. **Newly deviated:** Finding id=2 `Openssl Heap Overflow` was
   risk-accepted via the DefectDojo risk_acceptance API, creating
   risk acceptance id=1 ("RA-0001 Grafana Exposure Risk Adjustment").
   Shows as `poam-state: Deviated` in the May OSCAL output.

### Organic changes

The +8,472 net growth in total items is **not** from new CVE
discoveries between April and May. It is the documented import-scan
pile-up (ADR 0007 Risk #2): each monthly run creates a new
`ConMon YYYY-MM` engagement and re-imports the full Wazuh indexer
hit set into that engagement. DefectDojo's native dedup prevents
duplicate findings *within* an engagement but does not dedup
*across* engagements.

**Mitigation investigation (during May staging):** the `reimport-scan`
endpoint was evaluated as an alternative but does not support
cross-engagement reimport either. The documented workaround is to
keep a single rolling engagement per product, which is a structural
pipeline change deferred to the next ConMon milestone. For the
April->May diff narrative, the three intentional transitions are
the load-bearing signal; the volume growth is documented honestly
as a known scope limitation of the pilot.

## Reading the month-to-month diff

The April->May transition demonstrates the operational rhythm of a
real ConMon program:

- Findings move through their lifecycle (Open -> Completed via
  re-scan verification)
- New findings appear continuously (the synthetic demo finding
  models a real between-cycle CVE discovery)
- Risk acceptance decisions get documented and applied (the DR
  vocabulary produces real state changes, not just paperwork)
- The boundary itself can evolve via SCRs (SCR-0001 in this cycle)

This is **the most important artifact** in the entire portfolio for
demonstrating ConMon fluency. Static one-shot snapshots show that
the operator *generated* a POA&M; the diff shows that the operator
understands it as a **living document** maintained on a recurring
cadence.

## Reviewer note on "what's staged vs organic"

Per ADR 0010 and the whole-project design spec §6.5, the three
state transitions above are **intentionally staged** so the diff
narrative is clean and reviewable in a portfolio context. A real
CSP's April->May diff would look similar in shape but would have
organic rather than staged transitions. The staging is documented
honestly rather than pretending to be organic -- the "what's real,
what's notional" table in the main writeup calls this out
explicitly.
