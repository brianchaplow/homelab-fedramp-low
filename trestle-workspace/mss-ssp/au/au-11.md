# au-11 - \[Audit and Accountability\] Audit Record Retention

## Control Statement

Retain audit records for [time period] to provide support for after-the-fact investigations of incidents and to meet regulatory and organizational information retention requirements.

## Control Assessment Objective

audit records are retained for [time period] to provide support for after-the-fact investigations of incidents and to meet regulatory and organizational information retention requirements.

## Control guidance

Organizations retain audit records until it is determined that the records are no longer needed for administrative, legal, audit, or other operational purposes. This includes the retention and availability of audit records relative to Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests, subpoenas, and law enforcement actions. Organizations develop standard categories of audit records relative to such types of actions and standard response processes for each type of action. The National Archives and Records Administration (NARA) General Records Schedules provide federal policy on records retention.

### This System

Audit record retention is implemented across three log categories. For PCAP, Arkime captures full packets to haccp's dedicated Samsung 990 EVO Plus 2TB NVMe at `/opt/arkime/raw`. The nightly rsync cron (0300) archives completed PCAP files to smokehouse 17TB NFS (`~bchaplow/pcap-archive/haccp/`), and a `find ... -mtime +90 -delete` sweep enforces the 90-day off-line retention window required by M-21-31 and the FedRAMP AU-11 baseline. The local Arkime `freeSpaceG=100` policy maintains available PCAP online capacity and auto-purges oldest local PCAP -- the smokehouse archive ensures those purged files remain retrievable for 90 days. For Prometheus metrics, retention is explicitly configured at 90 days via `--storage.tsdb.retention.time=90d` in `brisket-setup/monitoring/docker-compose.yml`. PBS on smoker LXC 300 retains daily VM snapshots (DC01, WS01, TheHive) and weekly snapshots to smokehouse 17TB NFS under the backup job policies documented in CLAUDE.md.

The honest gap for this control is in Wazuh alert and ELK Zeek log retention. `wazuh-alerts-4.x-*` indices are retained on brisket OpenSearch until disk is full -- no ILM policy, no rollover policy, and no explicit minimum-retention assertion. The current disk fill rate means records are retained for months to years in practice, satisfying 90+ days by default, but this is not a policy-backed guarantee. Similarly, `logs-zeek.haccp-default-*` on haccp has no explicit ILM lifecycle policy. Neither index type has a formally documented NARA-mapped off-line archive path for endpoint event records. These gaps are acknowledged; the practical retention is adequate but the policy formalization is pending ConMon roadmap work. Status is partial.
