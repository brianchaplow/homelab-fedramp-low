# cp-10 - \[Contingency Planning\] System Recovery and Reconstitution

## Control Statement

Provide for the recovery and reconstitution of the system to a known state within [organization-defined time period consistent with recovery time and recovery point objectives] after a disruption, compromise, or failure.

## Control Assessment Objective

- \[CP-10[01]\] the recovery of the system to a known state is provided within [time period] after a disruption, compromise, or failure;

- \[CP-10[02]\] a reconstitution of the system to a known state is provided within [time period] after a disruption, compromise, or failure.

## Control guidance

Recovery is executing contingency plan activities to restore organizational mission and business functions. Reconstitution takes place following recovery and includes activities for returning systems to fully operational states. Recovery and reconstitution operations reflect mission and business priorities; recovery point, recovery time, and reconstitution objectives; and organizational metrics consistent with contingency plan requirements. Reconstitution includes the deactivation of interim system capabilities that may have been needed during recovery operations. Reconstitution also includes assessments of fully restored system capabilities, reestablishment of continuous monitoring activities, system reauthorization (if required), and activities to prepare the system and organization for future disruptions, breaches, compromises, or failures. Recovery and reconstitution capabilities can include automated mechanisms and manual procedures. Organizations establish recovery time and recovery point objectives as part of contingency planning.

### This System

The technical capability to recover both in-boundary GRC VMs to a known state from PBS snapshots is implemented and documented. The recovery procedure is specified in `runbooks/restore-from-pbs.md`: the operator stops the broken VM, preserves the config file, runs `qmrestore pbs-smokehouse:backup/vm/<VMID>/<ISO-timestamp> <VMID> --storage local-lvm --force` (in-place disk rewrite that preserves the MAC address pinning), starts the VM, and validates recovery with the automated smoke check scripts (`./pipelines.sh smoke-dojo`, `./pipelines.sh smoke-regscale`). MAC address pinning (dojo `BC:24:11:DE:F0:01`, regscale `BC:24:11:DE:F0:02`) is documented in `deploy/proxmox/README.md` to ensure cloud-init state and UFW rules survive restore without NIC identity churn.

The RTO target is 4 hours from detection of disruption to a passing smoke check. This is empirically supported by the 11m11s qmrestore runtime recorded in ADR 0005 (`docs/adr/0005-pbs-backup-gap-and-automount-fix.md`) during the 2026-04-08 operational verification. The RPO is 24 hours, bounded by the nightly PBS backup jobs (02:00 pitcrew, 02:30 smoker). Reconstitution steps specific to each service are documented: `deploy/regscale/reset-admin-password.sh` handles admin credential re-seeding for RegScale, and `deploy/defectdojo/post-install.sh` is an idempotent seed script for DefectDojo SLA and product definitions.

This control is rated `partial` because the RTO and RPO commitments have not been formally captured in a policy artifact predating this SSP, the planned restore drill per `runbooks/restore-from-pbs.md` has not yet been executed to empirically validate end-to-end recovery readiness, and brisket and haccp (bare-metal hosts) have no VM-level recovery procedure.

#### Implementation Status: partial
