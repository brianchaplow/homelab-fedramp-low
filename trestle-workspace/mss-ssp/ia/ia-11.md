# ia-11 - \[Identification and Authentication\] Re-authentication

## Control Statement

Require users to re-authenticate when [circumstances or situations].

## Control Assessment Objective

users are required to re-authenticate when [circumstances or situations].

## Control guidance

In addition to the re-authentication requirements associated with device locks, organizations may require re-authentication of individuals in certain situations, including when roles, authenticators or credentials change, when security categories of systems change, when the execution of privileged functions occurs, after a fixed time period, or periodically.

### This System

Re-authentication in the Managed SOC Service is enforced through session TTL mechanisms, protocol-level session controls, and operator-defined triggering events.

SSH sessions to all in-boundary hosts do not persist indefinitely; OS-level idle timeout settings and SSH `ClientAliveInterval` / `ClientAliveCountMax` configuration terminate inactive sessions, requiring a new SSH connection and key-based re-authentication. The Wazuh API JWT expires after the configured TTL (900-second default); the pipeline client in `pipelines/common/wazuh.py` detects 401 responses and re-authenticates automatically to obtain a fresh token. Wazuh Dashboard interactive sessions expire on browser close or inactivity timeout. RegScale CE JWTs carry a 24-hour TTL per ADR 0003 and ADR 0006 Deviation 7; every pipeline invocation calls `POST /api/authentication/login` to obtain a fresh token, meaning re-authentication occurs per invocation by design. RegScale interactive sessions expire at 1440 minutes. Shuffle SOAR, OpenCTI, TheHive, Cortex, Velociraptor, and DefectDojo each enforce session expiry through their platform defaults; browser sessions expire on close or inactivity. Tailscale device re-authentication is required when device keys expire (Tailscale default: 180 days unless disabled by the account admin).

Operator-defined re-authentication triggers include: session timeout (per service platform defaults), security incident or suspected compromise, role or credential change, explicit operator logout, and Tailscale device key expiry. A known gap is that `ClientAliveInterval` and `ClientAliveCountMax` settings are not uniformly documented across all hosts, and no centralized session monitoring enforces stale-session termination.
