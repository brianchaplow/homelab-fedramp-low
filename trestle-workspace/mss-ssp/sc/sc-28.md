# sc-28 - \[System and Communications Protection\] Protection of Information at Rest

## Control Statement

Protect the [Selection (one or more): confidentiality; integrity] of the following information at rest: [information at rest].

## Control Assessment Objective

the [Selection (one or more): confidentiality; integrity] of [information at rest] is/are protected.

## Control guidance

Information at rest refers to the state of information when it is not in process or in transit and is located on system components. Such components include internal or external hard disk drives, storage area network devices, or databases. However, the focus of protecting information at rest is not on the type of storage device or frequency of access but rather on the state of the information. Information at rest addresses the confidentiality and integrity of information and covers user information and system information. System-related information that requires protection includes configurations or rule sets for firewalls, intrusion detection and prevention systems, filtering routers, and authentication information. Organizations may employ different mechanisms to achieve confidentiality and integrity protections, including the use of cryptographic mechanisms and file share scanning. Integrity protection can be achieved, for example, by implementing write-once-read-many (WORM) technologies. When adequate protection of information at rest cannot otherwise be achieved, organizations may employ other controls, including frequent scanning to identify malicious code at rest and secure offline storage in lieu of online storage.

### This System

Information at rest protection relies on OS-level access controls and filesystem permissions across all in-boundary hosts. Wazuh configuration and indexer data on brisket are protected by Linux DAC (root-owned `/var/ossec/`, Docker volume permissions). ELK indices on haccp are protected by filesystem permissions on `/opt/elk/` Docker volumes, with the Elasticsearch data directory accessible only to the `elasticsearch` service account. PBS backup snapshots on smoker LXC 300 are stored on the smokehouse NFS share at `10.10.20.10:/mnt/pbs-store/` with NFS export restrictions limiting access to the PBS LXC client IP. OpenCTI threat intelligence data and Wazuh Indexer data on brisket are stored in Docker volumes with root-owned filesystem permissions. DefectDojo and RegScale application data on dojo and regscale VMs are protected by OS-level DAC under their respective service accounts. Sensitive credentials are stored in `.env` files with restricted filesystem permissions (not a secrets manager) and are excluded from version control via `.gitignore`.

Gap: no full-disk encryption (FDE) is applied to any in-boundary host's boot or data drives. The WD SN720 2TB boot drive and Samsung 990 EVO Plus 2TB PCAP drive on haccp, all brisket drives, and all Proxmox VM disks are unencrypted at rest. PBS backup snapshots are stored without datastore-level encryption. This is a deliberate homelab trade-off -- the compensating factor is physical security of a home rack with no untrusted colocated users. The failure mode that catches backup integrity drift was demonstrated by ADR 0005, where a backup gap was detected, fixed, and the automount configuration hardened. Status is partial -- OS-level access controls protect information at rest; cryptographic protection (FDE or datastore encryption) is not implemented. See SC-28(1) for the planned remediation path.
