# sr-11 - \[Supply Chain Risk Management\] Component Authenticity

## Control Statement

- \[a.\] Develop and implement anti-counterfeit policy and procedures that include the means to detect and prevent counterfeit components from entering the system; and

- \[b.\] Report counterfeit system components to [Selection (one or more): source of counterfeit component; [external reporting organizations]; [personnel or roles]].

## Control Assessment Objective

- \[SR-11a.\]

  - \[SR-11a.[01]\] an anti-counterfeit policy is developed and implemented;
  - \[SR-11a.[02]\] anti-counterfeit procedures are developed and implemented;
  - \[SR-11a.[03]\] the anti-counterfeit procedures include the means to detect counterfeit components entering the system;
  - \[SR-11a.[04]\] the anti-counterfeit procedures include the means to prevent counterfeit components from entering the system;

- \[SR-11b.\] counterfeit system components are reported to [Selection (one or more): source of counterfeit component; [external reporting organizations]; [personnel or roles]].

## Control guidance

Sources of counterfeit components include manufacturers, developers, vendors, and contractors. Anti-counterfeiting policies and procedures support tamper resistance and provide a level of protection against the introduction of malicious code. External reporting organizations include CISA.

### This System

The MSS anti-counterfeit posture relies on direct-from-vendor purchasing as the primary control to prevent counterfeit hardware from entering the system. All Lenovo ThinkStation units were purchased directly from Lenovo; the Protectli VP2420 was purchased from protectli.com (manufacturer direct); the MokerLink 10G08410GSM was purchased through Amazon Business. Drives added during the 2026-04-07 rack consolidation (Samsung 990 EVO Plus, others) were purchased new in sealed manufacturer packaging. No gray-market, refurbished, or third-party reseller hardware was used. Asset tags (HOMELAB-001 through HOMELAB-005, HOMELAB-FW-001, HOMELAB-SW-001) are tracked in `inventory/overlay.yaml` as component identity anchors.

For software, all services are installed from vendor-signed repositories (Wazuh apt repo at `packages.wazuh.com`, Elastic apt repo at `artifacts.elastic.co`) with apt GPG key verification at install time. Docker images are pulled from official Docker Hub repositories. RegScale CE is installed from the MIT-licensed installer at `github.com/RegScale/community` (reviewed in ADR 0001). These installation channels provide software authenticity verification through cryptographic signing.

If a counterfeit component were identified, it would be reported to the original hardware vendor (Lenovo, Protectli, or MokerLink as applicable) and to CISA as the FedRAMP-relevant external reporting organization. The System Owner (Brian Chaplow) is the sole internal reporting role.

The gap is the absence of a formal anti-counterfeit policy document and the lack of Docker image digest pinning (images pulled by tag, not digest). No counterfeit components have been identified to date.
