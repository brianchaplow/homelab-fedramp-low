# sr-12 - \[Supply Chain Risk Management\] Component Disposal

## Control Statement

Dispose of [data, documentation, tools, or system components] using the following techniques and methods: [techniques and methods].

## Control Assessment Objective

[data, documentation, tools, or system components] are disposed of using [techniques and methods].

## Control guidance

Data, documentation, tools, or system components can be disposed of at any time during the system development life cycle (not only in the disposal or retirement phase of the life cycle). For example, disposal can occur during research and development, design, prototyping, or operations/maintenance and include methods such as disk cleaning, removal of cryptographic keys, partial reuse of components. Opportunities for compromise during disposal affect physical and logical data, including system documentation in paper-based or digital files; shipping and delivery documentation; memory sticks with software code; or complete routers or servers that include permanent media, which contain sensitive or proprietary information. Additionally, proper disposal of system components helps to prevent such components from entering the gray market.

### This System

The MSS disposal process covers three categories of components. For storage media, the System Owner's standing practice is NVMe `nvme format --ses=1` secure erase for NVMe/SSD drives and `shred -n 7` (DoD 7-pass) for spinning media before disposal or reuse. Physical destruction is used when secure erase is not feasible. Drives replaced during the 2026-04-07 rack consolidation (prior haccp boot drive, prior pitcrew drive, prior smoker drive) are subject to this practice; the disposition of these specific drives is noted in CLAUDE.md rack consolidation notes.

For VM disk images, decommissioned VMs are destroyed via `qm destroy {vmid}` (Proxmox bare-metal VMs) or `pct destroy {ctid}` (LXC containers), which deallocates the disk image from the Proxmox datastore. The PBS (Proxmox Backup Server on smoker LXC 300) chunk store retains backup data until the next garbage collection run. OpenCTI LXC 202 was decommissioned on 2026-03-18 via this process (documented in CLAUDE.md: "autostart off," migrated to brisket Docker).

For secret data and documentation, all secrets are stored in `.env` (gitignored, never committed to the repository). When a service is decommissioned, its `.env` entries are removed and credentials are rotated. The `deploy/regscale/reset-admin-password.sh` script provides a credential-wipe step as a disposal-adjacent control before any reconstitution.

The gap is the absence of a formal drive disposal log for the 2026-04-07 drive swap disposals and no formal disposal technique policy document. The disposition record is informal (CLAUDE.md notes). PBS chunk retention after VM destruction is not formally tracked. These gaps are acknowledged and accepted at the partial rating.
