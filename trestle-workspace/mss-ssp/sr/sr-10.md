# sr-10 - \[Supply Chain Risk Management\] Inspection of Systems or Components

## Control Statement

Inspect the following systems or system components [Selection (one or more): at random; at [frequency]; upon [indications of need for inspection]] to detect tampering: [systems or system components].

## Control Assessment Objective

[systems or system components] are inspected [Selection (one or more): at random; at [frequency]; upon [indications of need for inspection]] to detect tampering.

## Control guidance

The inspection of systems or systems components for tamper resistance and detection addresses physical and logical tampering and is applied to systems and system components removed from organization-controlled areas. Indications of a need for inspection include changes in packaging, specifications, factory location, or entity in which the part is purchased, and when individuals return from travel to high-risk locations.

### This System

All in-boundary hardware components are inspected upon indications of need -- specifically at time of initial deployment, during hardware change events (drive swaps, NIC additions, rack moves), and annually. The 2026-04-07 rack consolidation is the primary documented inspection event: all four compute nodes (brisket, haccp, pitcrew, smoker), the Protectli VP2420 firewall, and the MokerLink 10G08410GSM switch were physically inspected by the System Owner during disassembly, cabling, and rack installation. Three drive swaps were performed (haccp 2TB Samsung 990 EVO Plus PCAP drive, pitcrew +512GB, smoker +1TB); each drive was visually inspected for physical damage or tampering indicators before installation. No tampering indicators were observed.

Ongoing logical inspection is provided by Wazuh syscollector, which continuously inventories hardware state (CPU model, RAM, NIC MAC addresses) on all in-boundary agents. Unexpected hardware changes -- NIC MAC drift, memory reduction -- would surface in syscollector deltas and are queryable at `https://10.10.20.30:55000/syscollector/{agent_id}/hardware`. Wazuh syscheck (file integrity monitoring) provides complementary detection of software-level tampering on all hosts.

The gap is the absence of a formal inspection schedule, written checklist, or results record. The 2026-04-07 rack build inspection was not formally documented in a structured artifact; it is referenced in CLAUDE.md notes and the rack build design spec. No Wazuh alert rule is currently configured to specifically alert on unexpected hardware changes. These gaps are acknowledged and accepted at the partial rating.

#### Implementation Status: partial
