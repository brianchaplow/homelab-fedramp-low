# pe-14 - \[Physical and Environmental Protection\] Environmental Controls

## Control Statement

- \[a.\] Maintain [Selection (one or more): temperature; humidity; pressure; radiation; [environmental control]] levels within the facility where the system resides at [acceptable levels] ; and

- \[b.\] Monitor environmental control levels [frequency].

## Control Assessment Objective

- \[PE-14a.\] [Selection (one or more): temperature; humidity; pressure; radiation; [environmental control]] levels are maintained at [acceptable levels] within the facility where the system resides;

- \[PE-14b.\] environmental control levels are monitored [frequency].

## Control guidance

The provision of environmental controls applies primarily to organizational facilities that contain concentrations of system resources (e.g., data centers, mainframe computer rooms, and server rooms). Insufficient environmental controls, especially in very harsh environments, can have a significant adverse impact on the availability of systems and system components that are needed to support organizational mission and business functions.

### This System

Temperature is the environmental control actively monitored for this system. GPU temperature on brisket is monitored continuously via Prometheus scraping NVIDIA SMI metrics (`nvidia_smi_temperature_gpu{job="brisket-nvidia"}`) every 15 seconds, with Grafana evaluating the alert "GPU Thermal Critical -- Brisket Above 90C" (uid=dfihoiidr7k00c, soc-infrastructure group) on a 2-minute window and routing to `#infrastructure-alerts` via the `discord-infrastructure` Discord policy. The operational target is GPU temperature below 85C; the Grafana critical threshold is 90C. Brisket's RTX A1000 GPU is power-capped to 40W via `nvidia-power-cap.service` (persistent across reboot, ExecStop restores 50W) to maintain acceptable thermal levels under sustained Ollama inference load. The Phase 14 thermal hardening incident (2026-04-08) demonstrated this architecture is operational: an unbounded Ollama workload drove the GPU to 87C sustained; the Grafana alert triggered, the root cause (unbounded tier-1 Ollama calls) was corrected and the power cap applied, reducing temperature to 63C and fan speed from 66% to 39%. Ambient room temperature is maintained by the residential HVAC system at approximately 68-76 degrees F (20-24 degrees C). The 12U open-frame rack (passive convection, no enclosed cabinet) dissipates rack heat naturally and keeps rack component temperatures within acceptable ranges at the observed 200-340W total draw.

This implementation is partial because only GPU temperature is formally monitored with automated alerting. Ambient room temperature relies on residential HVAC without dedicated sensors or Prometheus-integrated alerting. Humidity, pressure, and radiation are not monitored -- the residential HVAC maintains acceptable humidity as a side effect of its operation, but no humidity sensor or alert exists. These monitoring gaps are an intentional residential-context scoping decision: the GPU thermal alert addresses the highest-risk thermal component in the system, and the residential HVAC addresses ambient conditions without requiring dedicated infrastructure monitoring. Cross-reference PE-13 (fire protection) for the relationship between GPU thermal management and fire-risk reduction.
