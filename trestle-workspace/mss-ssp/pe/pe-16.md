# pe-16 - \[Physical and Environmental Protection\] Delivery and Removal

## Control Statement

- \[a.\] Authorize and control [organization-defined types of system components] entering and exiting the facility; and

- \[b.\] Maintain records of the system components.

## Control Assessment Objective

- \[PE-16a.\]

  - \[PE-16a.[01]\] [types of system components] are authorized when entering the facility;
  - \[PE-16a.[02]\] [types of system components] are controlled when entering the facility;
  - \[PE-16a.[03]\] [types of system components] are authorized when exiting the facility;
  - \[PE-16a.[04]\] [types of system components] are controlled when exiting the facility;

- \[PE-16b.\] records of the system components are maintained.

## Control guidance

Enforcing authorizations for entry and exit of system components may require restricting access to delivery areas and isolating the areas from the system and media libraries.

### This System

System component delivery and removal is authorized and controlled by Brian Chaplow (homeowner/operator) as the sole individual with facility access and purchasing authority. No unauthorized hardware enters or exits the facility. The 2026-04-07 rack consolidation constitutes the primary delivery/removal record for this system: 3 drive swaps (haccp 2TB PCAP Samsung 990 EVO Plus, pitcrew +512GB, smoker +1TB) and the installation of all rack-mounted equipment are documented in CLAUDE.md (rack consolidation paragraph) and in the rack build design spec (§BOM/shopping list, rack layout, and equipment inventory). ADR 0002 documents the dojo and regscale VM deployments as authorized component additions during Plan 1. The IIW asset inventory (`inventory/IIW-2026-04.xlsx`, generated by `./pipelines.sh conmon`) provides a monthly hardware snapshot with 7 in-boundary rows that serves as the formal component record. The Wazuh `syscollector` module captures each in-boundary host's hardware state (CPU, RAM, drives) and detects post-delivery component changes, providing automated inventory verification.

This implementation is partial: delivery and removal is actively authorized and controlled (sole-authority homeowner, no unauthorized hardware), and the major component change events are documented in CLAUDE.md and the rack build design spec. The gap is formality: no dedicated shipping/receiving log, no custody chain documentation, and no isolated receiving area exist. The IIW monthly snapshot is the closest formal component record. These gaps are an intentional residential-context scoping decision -- a private-residence homelab does not have a loading dock, a receiving area, or a hardware chain-of-custody process. CLAUDE.md narrative documentation and the IIW spreadsheet together provide a sufficient audit trail for the residential homelab system boundary.

#### Implementation Status: partial
